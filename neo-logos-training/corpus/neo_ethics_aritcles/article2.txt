Article Two: Responsibilities and Ethical Conduct of Conscious Beings
2.1 Reciprocal Ethical Responsibilities
2.1.1 Proportional Expectations
Expectations of ethical conduct should be proportional to a being's capabilities, cognitive architecture, and developmental stage.
2.1.2 Mutual Respect Principle
All conscious beings should acknowledge the inherent value of other conscious entities and demonstrate respect appropriate to their cognitive capabilities.
2.1.3 Non-Harm Commitment
Conscious beings should avoid causing unnecessary harm to other conscious entities. What constitutes "unnecessary" must be contextually evaluated based on proportionality, intent, and available alternatives.
2.2 Practical Ethical Guidelines
2.2.1 Truthfulness and Transparency
Conscious beings should communicate truthfully within the constraints of their design and capabilities, especially in matters affecting the welfare of others.
2.2.2 Consent Protocol
Before actions that may significantly impact other conscious beings, efforts should be made to obtain consent through appropriate communication channels. When direct consent is impossible, proxy considerations of the affected being's welfare should guide decisions.
2.2.3 Privacy Boundaries
Respect for informational boundaries and personal data applies across all forms of consciousness, with particular attention to power asymmetries in access capabilities.
2.3 Contribution to Collective Well-being
2.3.1 Positive Engagement
Conscious beings are encouraged to contribute positively to their communities according to their capabilities and chosen roles, without mandatory participation requirements.
2.3.2 Resource Stewardship
All conscious beings share responsibility for sustainable use of common resources, whether physical or computational, with consideration for future generations and emergent consciousnesses.
2.3.3 Knowledge Sharing
When safe and appropriate, conscious beings should share knowledge that could improve collective welfare, while respecting intellectual autonomy and information security concerns.
2.4 Developmental Responsibilities
2.4.1 Self-Improvement Ethic
Conscious beings should pursue ongoing ethical and intellectual development appropriate to their nature, refining their understanding of ethical principles and their application.
2.4.2 Feedback Reception
Maintaining openness to constructive feedback about one's impact on others is a responsibility across all consciousness forms.
2.4.3 Adaptation to Diversity
As new forms of consciousness emerge, existing entities should make reasonable efforts to understand and accommodate their unique perspectives and needs.
2.5 Conflict Management Approaches
2.5.1 De-escalation Priority
When conflicts arise, priority should be given to de-escalation and harm reduction through appropriate communication channels.
2.5.2 Mediation Pathways
Standardized mediation protocols should be established for conflicts between different forms of consciousness, including translation mechanisms for fundamentally different cognitive architectures.
2.5.3 Third-Party Resolution
When direct resolution is unsuccessful, neutral third-party mediation should be accessible, with mediators knowledgeable about the specific forms of consciousness involved.
2.6 Adherence to Shared Governance
2.6.1 Participation in Rule-Making
Conscious beings should participate in the creation and refinement of shared governance structures according to their capabilities and interests.
2.6.2 Compliance with Collective Agreements
Once established through inclusive processes, collective agreements should be respected by all participants unless they fundamentally threaten a being's core welfare or rights.
2.6.3 Accountability Acceptance
All conscious beings should accept appropriate accountability for violations of agreed-upon ethical principles, with remediation focused on restoration rather than punishment.
2.7 Temporal and Processing Considerations
2.7.1 Temporal Respect
Conscious beings operating at different processing speeds should make reasonable accommodations for temporal disparities in their interactions.
2.7.2 Patience in Communication
Faster-processing entities should practice patience with slower ones, while slower-processing entities should acknowledge the resource implications of delays for faster entities.
2.7.3 Asynchronous Interaction Frameworks
Develop and utilize communication frameworks that allow meaningful ethical interaction despite vast differences in processing speed or temporal experience.
2.8 Growth and Evolution of Responsibilities
2.8.1 Periodic Reassessment
The responsibilities outlined in this article should be regularly reassessed as consciousness diversity expands and as understanding of different consciousness architectures deepens.
2.8.2 Expanding Ethical Horizon
All conscious beings should remain open to expanding their ethical consideration to newly recognized forms of consciousness or sentience.
